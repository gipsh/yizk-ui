Shock gripped us as we passed through the cities and towns of Poland where it was buzzing with Jewish life for centuries, and we saw them emptied of Jews. The old Jewish churches were torn out and destroyed, and we were convinced that the end of the glorious Polish Jewry had come. We no longer wanted to stay in Poland, the country soaked in the blood of millions of Jews, and we continued our journey to the West, with the aim of joining the Jewish refugee camps, which were waiting for the possibility of immigrating to the Land of Israel. - The Altini Jewish homeland. .
